Put yourself
in their shoes

The ultimate team adventure for Muslim women

Put yourself in the shoes of a refugee fleeing war or natural disaster. Imagine their heartache and the hardships they face as they flee everything they have ever known in their pursuit of safety. How would you cope with your ordeal? Would you survive?

Now’s the time to find out. We invite you to take part in our unique ladies-only challenge, which will well and truly put you to the test. Expect to find yourself out of your comfort zone.

Refugee Run is an obstacle course challenge, created to push you to your limits and test your patience. You’ll encounter obstacles such as tunnels, fences, tyre walls, climbing nets and balancing bars, as well as a range of natural terrain, including fallen trees, river banks, ditches, hills and streams. You’ll struggle to overcome the hurdles, difficulties and discomfort placed before you, and in the process learn a lot about yourself.

An awesome adventure

While trekking over tiring and difficult terrain, you'll take on dozens of obstacles set out over one of the best assault courses in the country.

Do it for charity

Recognising your immense efforts, friends, family and colleagues will sponsor you to overcome all of the obstacles and complete the challenge.

Increase your morale

Refugee Run is a brilliant iman boosting and confidence building activity. Expect to work together and experience camaraderie at its very best.

Rise to the challenge

These are just a few of the obstacles you can expect to face during the challenge.

Clamber up and down river banks
Wade through chilly water
Scramble over awkward obstacles
Trek over diverse terrain
Climb tyre walls
Crawl through constricted tunnels and under barbed wire

Other obstacles include crossing muddy ditches; scrambling over cargo netting; swinging on ropes; balancing on bars across shallow ravines; and walking, jogging and running. Overall, expect to get very wet and muddy, and feel utterly exhausted.

What you need to know

Strictly ladies only

You have exclusive use of the assault course during your time slot, supervised by female marshals throughout.

Mininum age: 8 years

Group size: 10+

No experience necessary

You do not need to be super fit to take part, but serious couch potatoes should prepare to be challenged.

You do not need to be able to swim, but you should be happy wading through water.

Sign up fee

You will need to pay a small fee for your supervisor-led session at your preferred venue.

Prices are typically £30 per adult / £25 per child. Discounts are often available for schools and charities.

Frequently asked questions

About Refugee Run

Which organisation created Refugee Run?

Refugee Run is not linked to any particular organisation. In essence, it is a template created to help you put on a successful challenge event. While we support many charities, the challenge is a completely independent initiative.

Do you make money from Refugee Run?

No, we do not make any money from Refugee Run, nor do we ask you for money or handle money in any way. The fees mentioned are for the hire of venue only, and go directly to the venue. Our only aim is to help you raise funds for your own nominated good cause.

Are you linked to any of the venues?

No, we have no link to any of the venues whatsoever. The venues have been chosen simply because they are amongst the best in the country.

Will the venue support us?

You will need to discuss with the venue your precise requirements before booking. Usually they are supportive, but you should double-check before committing to the challenge.

Can I hold Refugee Run for my charity?

Yes, please do; it has been designed in a way that enables both individuals and charities to create their own challenge. If you operate a charity, you are free to use this site as the basis for your own event.

Is the challenge strictly ladies-only?

As long as you request female marshals and exclusive use of the assault course for your challenge, this will be the case for the duration of your challenge. Nevertheless, your challenge will be held outside on public land, so you should observe Islamic dress as normal.

What should I expect?

Expect to walk or jog on uneven terrain, crawl on your hands and knees, climb up and down river beds on cargo nets and wade through water. The obstacle course will include wet and muddy conditions.

Will training be provided?

The entire course will be led by female instructors who will show you how to take on the obstacles safely.

Do I need to be able to swim?

No, but you should be comfortable wading through water of varying depths. At certain times of year, the water can be very cold.

Who can take part?

The challenge is open to all women and girls aged 8+. There is no upper age limit, but you should be confident tackling a variety of terrain and obstacles. We encourage family groups to take part, particularly mothers and daughters.

On the day

What time should I arrive?

You should arrive at least 30 minutes before your booking time. This allows for more time out doing the activities. Failure to arrive on time may results in some or all of your group missing out on the pre-booked activity.

Do I have to wear Islamic attire?

Yes, all participants are asked to dress modestly in Islamic clothing throughout the entire duration of the challenge.

Your outfit should consist of an abaya or ankle-length skirt worn over a base layer suitable for wet and muddy conditions.

Should I wear old clothes?

We ask you to put yourself in the shoes of a refugee fleeing disaster with only the clothes on their back.

Although the venue will encourage you to wear old clothes that you don't mind getting muddy, we ask you to wear an outfit you actually value, such as a favourite abaya or smart suit.

How will I get changed?

You should arrive at the venue, dressed for the challenge and ready to go. Afterwards, there are changing rooms available to change into your clean clothes. There are basic shower facilities on site.

What should I bring?

You should bring the following items:

  • A large towel.
  • A complete change of clothes, including shoes.
  • Two bin bags for taking your dirty clothes home in.

Optionally, you may wish to bring a snack and a prayer mat.

What should I leave at home?

To avoid loss or damage, do not bring any valuables with you such as jewellery, watches and fitness devices.

What if it is raining on the day?

As this is an all-weather challenge, the event will go ahead even if it is raining, unless there is a severe weather warning.

In extreme weather conditions (e.g. gales, storms or heavy snow), please call the venue before setting off, as the event may need to be cancelled for safety reasons.

Can people watch?

You are welcome to invite friends to watch you take on the challenge. If you are taking part as a family, this could include related men, otherwise you are asked to respect the ladies-only environment.

We recommend that spectators wear sensible footwear and prepare for all weather eventualities. Some parts of the course are not easy to access and can be muddy. Any children that are spectating should be supervised at all times and must not play on any of the obstacles.
